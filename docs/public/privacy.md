# Privacy Policy

**Upmarket**
Effective date: 1 June 2026

Upmarket processes your documents entirely on your Mac. Nothing you convert is ever transmitted to a server.

---

## What we do not collect

- Document contents or converted text
- File names or paths
- Passwords entered to unlock documents
- Usage events or analytics
- Advertising identifiers
- Location data

---

## What is stored locally

All of the following stays on your Mac only.

| Data | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Tour completion flag | Prevents the first-launch tour from repeating |
| Shelf position | Remembers shelf corner between launches |
| Free conversions remaining | Tracks trial usage |
| Pack credit ledger | Records purchased and consumed document pack credits |
| Downloaded AI models | On-device AI for complex document conversion |

---

## Network access

The App makes network requests for three purposes only.

1. **AI model download.** When you choose to download AI models, the app fetches model files. No document data is included. Downloads can be skipped; the app works without them.

2. **App Store purchases.** Purchase verification and restore use Apple's standard App Store APIs. Upmarket has no payment server.

3. **Feature availability.** The app fetches a small public configuration file to check which features are available for your device language. The request contains no document data or app-specific identifiers.

Conversion is always performed offline. The AI runs entirely on your Mac.

---

## Crash reports

Upmarket has no third-party crash reporting or analytics SDK.

If your Mac is set to share diagnostics with developers, Apple may collect crash reports and make them available through Apple's developer tools. This is controlled by your macOS privacy settings and is subject to [Apple's privacy policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/).

If you use Help > Report a Problem, the app creates a local diagnostic bundle. It contains app version, build number, macOS version, hardware class, locale, last conversion stage, error code, correlation identifier, and internal health check results. It does not include document contents, extracted text, passwords, or file paths. You review and approve it before anything is sent.
